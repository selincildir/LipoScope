What LipoScope adds

The Atlas connects the consensus and data-element concepts to PubMed-wide evidence, cohort strictness, comparator strategy, diagnostic-noise auditing, gap prioritization, and REDCap-ready export.

How to use this safely

Use the Atlas to plan and document reproducible research cohorts, then verify final forms against the official CCRF spreadsheets, protocol requirements, ethics approval, and local clinical governance.

Important distinction: LipoScope does not reproduce or replace the official CCRF data dictionaries. It provides a Delphi/CCRF-aware planning and export layer so researchers can see why fields are needed and how missing fields affect cohort interpretation.

Gene and Literature Evidence Checker

Check whether a gene symbol or recognised alias has appeared in the PubMed-wide lipoedema/lipedema literature layer or maps to predefined lipoedema-relevant biology. This supports cohort design, exploratory CCRF-style molecular fields, and hypothesis triage; it does not validate biomarkers, classify pathogenicity, or diagnose lipoedema.

Use this when: you are designing a genetics, biomarker, imaging, or mechanistic cohort and want to know whether a candidate gene symbol or recognised alias is mentioned in the lipoedema/lipedema literature layer, maps to predefined biology context, has differential/overlap relevance, or has no current LipoScope match. Outputs include exact PubMed query strings, supporting paper links, biology-context categories, and a not validated for clinical diagnosis interpretation badge.

Paste gene symbols

One gene symbol per line, or comma/semicolon separated. LipoScope searches the PubMed-wide lipoedema/lipedema evidence layer using recognised gene symbols and aliases, then maps recognised genes to predefined lipoedema-relevant biological programs. Non-gene identifiers are searched only as exact literature text and are not mapped to genes.

How to interpret results

Literature mention means the gene symbol or a recognised alias appears in the lipoedema/lipedema PubMed-wide evidence layer used by LipoScope; this is not biomarker validation.
Biology-context match means the gene maps to a predefined lipoedema-relevant program, such as adipose biology, lymphatic/vascular biology, ECM/fibrosis, hormone biology, pain/inflammation, connective tissue, or RAS-MAPK.
Differential/overlap context means the gene may be more useful for identifying mimics, overlap conditions, comparator groups, or stratification variables than for defining a core lipoedema cohort.

Standardized Cohort Builder

Build Delphi-aligned and CCRF-aware lipoedema research cohort rules for genetics, imaging, biomarker, treatment-response, registry, and diagnostic-accuracy studies. This section does not diagnose; it helps reduce diagnostic noise, document differential review, define comparator groups, and export reproducible cohort blueprints.

Source table: Clarity, Agreement, Inclusion, Evidence, and More Evidence are the percentage values reported in Table 1 of the Delphi paper; LipoScope does not recalculate them. Research Use explains how each consensus statement can become a cohort variable, differential guardrail, comparator field, or validation endpoint.
Purpose: use the builder below when a study team needs a transparent case definition before recruitment, sequencing, imaging, sampling, treatment follow-up, or registry intake. The output is a cohort blueprint: inclusion rules, exclusion/stratification rules, comparator strategy, diagnostic-noise audit, required fields, and protocol language.
Evidence anchors: Lipedema World Alliance Delphi consensus for standardized CRF, diagnostic limitations, and future validation priorities; Lipedema Foundation CCRF for common data-element levels; physical assessment tools review for measurement-standardization gaps.
Use study codes only. Do not enter names, dates of birth, phone numbers, or clinical record numbers.

Cohort Design Gap Radar

Shows which cohort-building requirements need stronger planning before lipoedema studies can be compared across genetics, imaging, biomarkers, treatment response, registries, and diagnostic-accuracy research.

Purpose: use this before drafting a protocol or registry form. It does not say that a research area is unimportant; it shows where cohort design needs more explicit rules so future studies are easier to compare.
How it is built: LipoScope combines PubMed-wide topic counts, study-design terms, validation/reproducibility language, comparator terms, Delphi Table 1 evidence/additional-evidence signals, and CCRF-style minimum dataset domains. When live PubMed counts are unavailable, it uses the cached PubMed-wide Atlas layer and labels the source.
How to read it: rows are cohort requirements and columns are study types. Rose/pink cells indicate a High gap priority, amber/yellow cells indicate a Medium gap priority, and teal/green-blue cells indicate a Watch / plan explicitly priority. The detailed table below the grid gives the trigger, missing evidence, suggested study design, exact PubMed query, and source anchors.
Evidence anchors: Delphi consensus future directions; Lipedema Foundation CCRF; diagnostic imaging systematic review; clinical/therapeutics systematic review; physical assessment tools review.

Lipoedema Minimum Dataset Builder

A proposed Delphi-aligned, CCRF-crosswalked planning dataset for future lipoedema studies, designed to make cohorts, genetics studies, imaging studies, biomarker studies, registries, and interventions easier to compare.

Purpose: use this builder to choose a minimum set of fields for a planned study and export them as CSV or REDCap-ready variables. Required fields map to core phenotype, diagnosis-source, anthropometry, differential-review, and governance concepts. Recommended and optional fields support study-specific depth.
Source alignment: LipoScope maps fields to Delphi statement domains and to CCRF-style collection levels using a planning crosswalk. Many fields correspond to official CCRF data-dictionary concepts, while genetics, omics, governance, and protocol-export variables include LipoScope-specific research extensions. This is not a replacement for the official CCRF data dictionaries; teams should verify final forms against the CCRF spreadsheets and their approved protocol.

📖 About This Resource

LipoScope supports research into lipoedema (lipedema), a chronic painful adipose-tissue disorder where delayed diagnosis, inconsistent case definitions, small cohorts, limited objective biomarkers, and fragmented genetics evidence still slow progress.

The public default Browse collection contains 200 lipoedema/lipedema papers spanning 2008–2026, combining a manually curated seed with a PubMed-expanded metadata layer. Each record includes topic and study-type classification, source links, open-access figure lookup where full-text access supports it, and optional local PDF-based figure preview in the browser. Uploaded PDFs can be kept on the same device for the same local workspace without being added to the public paper database or shared link payload.

The Research Atlas extends Browse into research operations: PubMed-wide evidence mapping, candidate gene and biomarker ranking, mechanism hypotheses, Delphi-aligned diagnostic consensus fields, CCRF-aware minimum dataset structure, standardized cohort building, diagnostic-noise auditing, gene-literature evidence checking, cohort design-gap prioritization, and REDCap-friendly exports. The Analytics tab separates whole-field PubMed signals from the visible Browse workspace, and Live Search lets you add new PubMed records with one click.

A Browser-Local Research Workbench for Lipoedema

LipoScope turns a fragmented lipoedema literature into a practical research command centre. You can browse the 200-paper public corpus, add fresh PubMed records, inspect structured paper summaries and figures, compare your local curation with the wider PubMed landscape, and then move into Atlas tools for diagnostic standardization, cohort-rule drafting, genetics, biomarkers, mechanisms, and study design.

The app is built for reusable research rather than one-off reading. It helps a diagnostic accuracy study define comparable case fields, a genetics study set strict phenotype and comparator rules, an imaging or biomarker project control comorbidity noise, and a registry or intervention study export common data elements. No registration is required, and sensitive local inputs such as phenotype fields, notes, and PDFs remain on your device unless you choose to export them.

▶ Lipoedema Biology Explainer Video

From symptom pattern to testable biology

This animated scientific explainer frames lipoedema as a multi-system research problem: sex-hormone timing and inherited susceptibility may interact with adipose remodeling, ECM/fibrosis, lymphatic or vascular dysfunction, inflammation, pain, and treatment-response heterogeneity.

  • Clinical pattern first: symmetrical limb enlargement, pain, bruising, heaviness, and foot sparing are placed beside the tissue biology they may reflect.
  • Mechanisms you can test: adipocyte hypertrophy, ECM remodelling, lymphatic or microvascular stress, inflammation, and pain signalling are translated into measurable study domains.
  • From gene to cohort: candidate genes and biomarkers are treated as leads for matched phenotyping, imaging, serum/adipose assays, and longitudinal treatment outcomes.
